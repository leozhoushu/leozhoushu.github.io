Problems

There are a lot of international students here in the United States. Arriving in a new country, they are more easily to get stressed out due to language problem, heavy workload etc.

 

Stress arises as result of certain factors and then affects student's behavior and mood. So accordingly there are two potential ways to cope with it.

 

Final Solution Overview

User Research

First-Round Interview

To see which solution is more feasible, I did the first round of interview. This interview focused on what causes stress and what these students found relaxing. It turns out that students have unique ways to deal with pressure problem. I did brainstorming afterwards and provided interviewers with 8 different ideas to choose from. Their choices are distributed which also indicates it's hard to find an universal stress-relieving method that applies to everybody.

 

 

 

First-Round Findings

Second-Round Interview

This round of interview focused on why international students feel stressful by learning their everyday life. One prevalent answer is they usually feel unorganized. So I kept on exploring what applications they are using now to organize daily activities and what's the problem within the process. Besides, I paid attention to international student's posts on social media to better understand what's the feature of this unique group.

 

"I'm busy everyday but I don't feel productive, I don't know where my time goes."

1) Set up a ceremony

We decided to make this process more seamless by including all of the fields on one screen and having a progress indicator at the top.

2) Receive notifications & Provide feedback

From our user tests we learned this was the most important part of the user flow. We had to make this as quick and as easy as possible or else users would not respond to the survey prompt. So we simplified the three questions our client outlined into one overarching "How did the ceremony go?".
 
A user just needs to tap a smiley button and they are done. However, to allow for more qualitative responses we added attribute buttons that are optional along with an optional comments field. The attributes can be customized. We used our affinity wall findings to create the stock buttons content.

3) Analyze Data

Finally, we visualized the data from the survey responses in a dashboard. A user is provided an overview of all of the ceremonies they attend. Users can also filter the data to look for patterns and trends.
 
Users can also drill into the trend lines of individual ceremonies. The app puts data into the hands of employees.
