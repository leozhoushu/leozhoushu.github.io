Ceremony

The goal of the mobile app is to empower employees to rate the success of reoccurring meetings (ceremonies) at work. The app then provides data on organization's meetings that will help them improve their productivity.

Our client Tom is currently writing the book Ceremony which he hopes will improve productivity in the workplace. He will be consulting companies on his book and wanted to also build a companion app to help companies implement the book's teachings. In his vision, it should be a pretty simple app: asking people three related questions after each meeting, quick and easy. However what we concerned more is how to empower people to use this app. Rating a ceremony can be one extra work, we need to dig out or stimulate user's inside motivation.

  • Ceremony
    Ceremony is any repeated social interaction between two or more people, like meeting, wedding, friend gathering etc. In our case, we mainly focused on meetings.

So, what makes a good meeting?

To better understand uers, we first did our own user research. We interviewed 8 different people and asked them questions about the meetings they attend. We talked to people who work in a wide range of industries including academic, religious, financial, construction, co-op housing, consulting, and technology.

We created an affinity wall based on the interviews. Here's attributes we foungd apply to a productive meeting.

  • Participant engagement in the meeting is key
  • People should feel encouraged to contribute
  • Good leadership matters
  • Good meeting structure increases productivity
  • Positive group culture helps meetings evolve

Final Solution

The whole process consists of four parts:
 

  • 1) Set up a ceremony
  • 2) Receive notification
  • 3) Provide anonymous feedback
  • 4) Analyze data

1) Set up a ceremony

We decided to make this process more seamless by including all of the fields on one screen and having a progress indicator at the top.

2) Receive notifications & 3) Provide feedback

From our user tests we learned this was the most important part of the user flow. We had to make this as quick and as easy as possible or else users would not respond to the survey prompt. Imagine if you have 10 meetings a week in the app and if each survey takes 5 minutes to complete. That's almost an hour spent every week responding to surveys. People don't have time for that. So we simplified the three questions our client outlined into one overarching "How did the ceremony go?".
 
A user just needs to tap a smiley button and they are done. However, to allow for more qualitative responses we added attribute buttons that are optional along with an optional comments field. The attributes can be customized. We used our affinity wall findings to create the stock buttons content.

4) Analyze data - Overview

Finally, we visualized the data from the survey responses in a dashboard. A user is provided an overview of all of the ceremonies they attend and can also drill into the trend lines of individual ceremonies. Users can filter the data to look for patterns and trends. They can say "I want to see all of the attributes for ceremonies that are trending up."
 
The app puts data into the hands of employees. The data speaks for itself: Ceremony makes it transparent. Bad data empowers positive change. Good data allows employees to see what is working well.
